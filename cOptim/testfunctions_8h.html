double rosenbrock ( const column_vector &  x)

Rosenbrock's function.

Rosenbrock's valley is a classic optimization problem, also known as Banana function. The global optimum is inside a long, narrow, parabolic shaped flat valley. To find the valley is trivial, however convergence to the global optimum is difficult and hence this problem has been repeatedly used in assess the performance of optimization algorithms.

Global minimum: f(x)=0; x(i)=1, i=1:n.

Bounds: -2.048 < xi < 2.048

Schwefel's function.

Schwefel's function is deceptive in that the global minimum is geometrically distant, over the parameter space, from the next best local minima. Therefore, the search algorithms are potentially prone to convergence in the wrong direction.

Global minimum: f(x)=0; x(i)=420.9687, i=1:n

Bounds: -500 < xi < 500
